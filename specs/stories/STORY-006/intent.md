The operator runs `--transform` against a cACV CSV that contains one or more customers. Before the portfolio JSON is written, the system needs to infer which industry vertical each customer belongs to — for example "Pharma/Life Sciences", "Manufacturing", or "Healthcare" — so that the `industry_insights` blocks in the portfolio JSON are correctly grouped and the dashboard left pane can display meaningful industry-level aggregations. This story implements that inference deterministically, without AI, using a combination of customer name heuristics and the product portfolio fingerprint (which SAP L1/L2 solution areas are active in the contract) encoded in a lookup table in `src/lib/industryInference.js`. The inferred industry is written to `entity:customer.industry` and used to populate the `industry_insights[].industry` keys and aggregate the contract values across customers in the same industry.

A successful completion means that every `portfolio.json` produced by `--transform` contains a non-null `industry` field on every customer entry and a correctly populated `industry_insights[]` array at the portfolio root, with each entry's `aggregated_contracts` reflecting the sum of `annual_contract_value`, `budget_contract_value`, and `consumed_contract_value` across all L3 contract months for customers assigned to that industry. After this story is built, the dashboard left pane in STORY-005 can render the full industry list on load, and the --analyze AI pipeline in STORY-003 can assign Step 5 (industry insights) to the correct industry group without re-deriving the assignment.
